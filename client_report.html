Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?
I started by comparing color palette samples and looking at examples of how websites would look with different colors and fonts. Then I picked images that matched the vibe I wanted. Since my site is about cooking, I just looked up easy, cheap recipes for college students.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?
I talked to my developer on the first day, we exchanged contact info, and I sent him my site plan. He later sent me the final result, which I was happy with overall, even though one child page was missing because a junior developer couldn’t be reached.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?
Yes, the lead developer met my expectations. It was just a little disappointing that one child page was missing due to the unreachable junior developer.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?
Next time, I’ll check in more often and communicate better with my team to make sure everything gets done.

I emailed both junior developers and also talked to them during class. One of them was unreachable, but we were able to do their part ourselves.

How did you handle publishing your client’s site and sharing it with the client?
Publishing went great. I sent the link to my client, although a bit late due to the delays. And I asked for feedback, but didn’t get a response.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?
It was confusing at first, but I eventually understood how to use it to a certain extent.

General Experience Report

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?
The hardest part was communication, especially with my client. The site plan was a little vague, so I had to figure out a lot on my own. I overcame it by asking for more details and using examples.

What did you enjoy most about this project?
I enjoyed creating my site plan and seeing the finished websites. It was fun picking the color palette, fonts, and images.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?
I’d try to connect more with my team and make everyone feel more comfortable communicating. I’d also ask more questions when needed.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.
I would say now I definitely feel more confident because now I know how to handle roles, use GitHub, and work through issues as a team.
